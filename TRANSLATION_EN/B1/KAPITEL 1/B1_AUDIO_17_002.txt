- At the beginning of the 19th century, many people in Brazil still emigrated.
- However, in 1828 the then-emperor of Brazil, Dom Pedro the Second, decided that no European immigrants may come to Brazil anymore.
- At that time groups of about a hundred Luxembourgers were on their way there.
- They had sold everything in Luxembourg to start a new life in Brazil, but when they sailed to Bremen, that no longer worked.
- They had to return to Luxembourg, and they had even less.
- The Luxembourg government helped them in part, and they got a place where they could settle.
- Thus, on the border of the municipalities Grëissbous, Wal, and Heischent, a new village arose, which at the time was called Neider- or New Brazil in 1828.
- Today it is called Gréiwels and belongs to the municipality Wal.
- Guy Helminger, a Luxembourgish writer, wrote the German-language novel "Nei Brasilien," which tells the history of immigrants in Luxembourg and immigrants who eventually lived in New Brazil.
