- Public petitions in Maddenker can try out to see if a topic is personally important to the citizen.
- For that, he must make a formal request to the petition committee of the Chamber of Deputies.
- There it is checked whether the petition meets the criteria to be published.
- A petition must always have a general interest as its topic and may not address personal or individual problems.
- It must also be submitted with the correct form, which can be found on the Chamber's website.
- It must not be that the person who drafts the petition has had an English matriculation number ten years ago.
- If all of this is in order, the petition is posted online on the Chamber's site.
- For 42 days it can collect signatures.
- If within that period 4500 signatures are gathered, it could lead to a debate in the Chamber.
- The debate is broadcast on Chamber TV, and those interested can still follow it live in the Chamber.
- This is also possible for all other debates in the Chamber.
- On average, a new petition to the Chamber is submitted every day.
- The topic of traffic, for example, is something that is often addressed.
- Other topics would include the ban on peace celebrations, the legalization of cannabis, animal protection, mandatory euthanasia consultations, prison penalties for various medical treatments, and so on.
- Petitions to make the last Bavarian ruling more important, i.e., to make it a formal official statement, have so far the record for signatures.
- 14,700 copies.
- Above all, the petition against the ban on pedestrian rights has the most signatures.
