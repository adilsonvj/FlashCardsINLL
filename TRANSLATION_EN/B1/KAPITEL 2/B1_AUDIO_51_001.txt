- Read and listen to the event-text about Tröberfauer in the Hämelsmarsch.
- Every year at the end of the big vacation, many people look forward again to the traditional big fair in the city.
- For many people from the mouse, whether one sometimes steps over Tröberfuer, a baked fish, or a pastry is eaten.
- Tröberfauer has a long history, and is one of the oldest and most important traditions that is still celebrated today.
- On October 20, 1343, Jeanne de Blannen, Count of Luxembourg and King of Bamen, founded Tröberfauer.
- In the beginning it was a one-week market.
- Foreign merchants came to Luxembourg to buy and sell.
- That was good for the economy, but especially for the export of traditional items like Luxembourg wool.
- Tröberfauer traditionally always started on the day for the Robertl-Meeschter, i.e., August 23.
- At first Tröberfauer was organized on the Hällisch-Kiesch-Plato.
- In the past this place was called Schödpursch.
- Afterwards the name “Um Schöberfauer” or also “Schöbermös” emerged.
- Over time Tröberfauer continued to grow.
- Already in the 17th century it was planned on the Lamperzpürsch and definitively on the Glacis in 1893.
- In the 20th century more and more games came to the fair, such as Trieserat and Dachtabunnen.
- Slowly the fun factor at the fair became more important.
- But to this day one still finds market stalls with various goods for sale at the many attractions and in the restaurants.
- A tradition associated with Schöberfauer, and in general with the fair, is the tradition of the Hämelsmarsch.
- On the Scheißbunnen or the Kelebunnen one could formerly win a lamb.
- To show people what they could win and to invite them to the fair, the music association from the village together with the Schofsfjerd walked through the streets and played the melody of the Hämelsmarsch.
- Even today this tradition is kept, even if there is not always a lamb with it.
- Another song that is played today is “The Fair in the Village.”
