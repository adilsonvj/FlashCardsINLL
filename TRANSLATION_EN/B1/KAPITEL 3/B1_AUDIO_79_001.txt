- Mr. Hervé Klein works at the center of the 112.
- And this is where he talks about the Cgtist from the Funderettungsbänkster.
- Mr. Klein, in 2013 the entire system of the Rettungsbänkster Hëtz-Lëtzebursch was reformatted.
- Can you describe to us the biggest changes and say what prompted the change?
- Yes, of course, it was a very large form, but I’ll try to grasp it.
- So, on the one hand, in 2013 all Rettungsbänkster for Luxembourg were closed at the Cgtist in Sommer.
- This means civil protection, voluntary pompions, state vocational pompions, flight rescue pompions, and the Sarmü.
- On the other hand, from 2020 onward, with the CNS to Kasbrüch, a final size is created where the most important elements of the rescue system are regrouped.
- There you have, for example, Vocational pompjons, Central 112, and Night Administration and Formation Centers.
- All those structures are spread across different places in the country.
- It was difficult for communication in the neighborhoods.
- Now, where all that is, it’s easier, and the administrative overhead isn’t as heavy as before.
- Can you perhaps go a little into what the main missions and responsibilities of the Cgtist are?
- It’s hard to say what the main missions are, because it’s always about saving lives.
- And in that sense they are all important.
- It can be said that the mission of the Cgtist is to help people in urgent need in Luxembourg.
- A fire extinguished, to help in a major accident, medically assist, and to bring to the hospital, and so on.
- We help not only Luxembourg, but also with disasters abroad.
- Prevention and training are also important.
- It’s always better when it can lead to a good emergency response.
- For that, people need to know how to protect themselves or how to prevent it.
- In France and Luxembourg there must be serious emergency lines, the number 112.
- That line can also be searched from a young age at school, and across Europe the same number applies.
- Do you use it that much?
- Oh yes, in that moment you realize you have about 1400 emergencies per day.
- A team of outstanding volunteers maintains permanent emergency readiness.
- All calls are organized threefold, and the alarm is released when needed, and the right rescue team is sent out.
- On average there are about 200 interventions made.
- It’s not always about emergencies when people call 112.
- It could also be about whether you want to know which doctors, pharmacies, or hospitals you should contact for first aid instructions.
- Therefore the 112 emergency call is not ignored.
- The most serious organized free first aid in Luxembourg in German, French, and English for almost all year across the country.
- Why is it so valuable to invest in these cures?
- Every day there are accidents, and in such a situation it’s important to act correctly.
- Anyone can do that.
- Therefore we do many courses in different places at different times and in different languages.
- In these courses they learn how to behave properly in an accident, and how to provide proper first aid until professionals arrive, which are rescue structures.
- Courses last 16 hours and finally serve as a test.
- When you complete it, you receive a diploma valid for five years.
