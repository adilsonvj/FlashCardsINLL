- During morning rounds I help real patients with getting up and with undressing or dressing.
- Then I have to wash patients or help them wash their teeth, put on makeup, or comb their hair.
- We help people with everything they cannot manage themselves.
- We also have to take patients to the toilet or change diapers.
- That isn’t so nice, but it’s also part of the job.
- Every two hours I turn the patients in bed.
- Sometimes I also assist patients, and I have to encourage many more people to drink enough and to remind them of their medications.
- As an A2 assistant, we may not give patients medication ourselves, but we must ensure that they take the medications prescribed by the doctor regularly.
- In the hospital there are also many people who can’t get up anymore.
- Every two hours we must reposition the people to prevent them from getting sores.
- That is extremely exhausting and, on top of that, a stabbing pain in the corner.
- When I prepare patients for their operations, I have to shave them or wash them.
- Before and after a operation the patients are usually extremely excited, and I try to talk to them and calm them a little.
- In the afternoon there are many patients around, so we have to make the bed fresh and prepare the care items for the next patient.
- I am always happy when someone can go home again.
- That means they are back to health, and then we also get cookies or a small gift as thanks for the work.
